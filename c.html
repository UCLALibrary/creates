C: Concept Map Concept Map the Introduction

You can learn so much about an article from the introduction! Here's a step-by-step process for getting the most out of the article intro:

How to read the introduction:

Defining key terms you find in the introduction will help you identify uncertainties and set you up for a better understanding the article. Identifying the research question is essential for grasping the article as a whole. Identifying experimental variables and their relationships is key to going beyong definitions and figuring out how concepts interact.

  1. Define the key terms and read up on any unfamiliar concepts.
  2. Identify what was already known about the topic and what research questions or problems have yet to be addressed.
  3. What are the key experimental variables?
  4. What are the goals and/or hypotheses of the paper? In other words, how is this paper proposing to fill in the gaps in our understanding?
  5. See if you can identify general methodologies or experimental approaches that were used to answer the questions or test the hypotheses.
  6. Make note of any hypotheses, because you will need to come back to these later.

Draw your concept map of the introduction

  1. Get out a large, blank piece of paper and some pens and pencils.
  2. Start with the biggest picture concepts at the center or top of your paper and build out related ideas.
  3. Connect the concepts by lines and label the lines with one or a few linking words.
    • There may be links that run in different directions to connect ideas in different ways
    • There is not one right way to do concept maps, but advanced maps have more branches and connections

Guide & Enable Tips & strategies

"I find it helpful to start concept mapping on Post-It notes so I can move them around. Then I transfer everything to a single sheet of paper." - a successful learner

Tips

  • Have lots of scratch paper handy.
  • Read the article once all the way through before starting your map.
  • Prefer to work on a computer? Try Cmap, a free concept mapping software.
  • The main purpose of the introduction is often to show the relationships between current knowledge and the research question. Make sure your concept map shows this!

Your turn!

Make your own concept map! Use this checklist to make sure your concept map includes everything it needs.

  • Concepts in order from broad to narrow.
  • Linking word(s) that explain relationships between concepts.
